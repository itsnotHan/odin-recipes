Pan-Seared Garlic Butter Steak

Description

Super easy pan-seared steak that has a garlic rosemary-infused butter that makes it taste like a steakhouse quality meal. Seared and caramleized on the outside, and so juicy and tender on the inside.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs New York Steaks
  • 1/2 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper, freshly grounded
  • 2 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and quartered
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
    •

    Steps

    1. Throughly pat steak dry with paper towels. Just before cooking, generously season with 1 1/2 tsp salt and 1 tsp black pepper
    2. Heat the cast iron pan until hot then add 1/2 Tbsp oil over medium heat swirling to coat.
    3. Once the oil is very hot, add steaks to skillet.
    4. Sear steaks on first side for 4 minutes until brown crust has formed, then flip and do the same. Use tongs to turn steak on its sides to render the white fat and sear edges (1-min per edge).
    5. Reduce heat to medium and add 2 Tbsp butter, garlic cloves, and romemary to pan. Spoon the butter sauce over steak, continue doing until the steak is about 5-10 degrees from desired doneness.
    6. Transfer steak to a cutting board, loosely cover and rest 10 min before slicing into 1/2" strips to serve.
    7. Finally, enjoy delicious steak!
